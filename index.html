Research Overview

I am broadly interested in fluid dynamic problems at the interface of material, biological, thermal and environmental engineering. I utilize advanced numerical techniques to evaluate dynamic systems and to uncover fundamental fluid-structure interactions. My research aims to solve critical energy and environmental issues in our society.
01

Numerical Modeling and Validation

Focusing on the rigorous validation of numerical models against experimental benchmarks. Assessing the accuracy of RANS and LES simulations in predicting aerodynamic coefficients and flow topologies for complex marine geometries.

ANSYS Fluent RANS/LES Validation
02

Fluid-Structure Interactions (FSI)

Investigating the dynamic coupling between fluid flow and flexible or moving structures. My research explores how oscillatory flows impact structural integrity and performance, particularly for marine propulsion systems.

FSI Steady Flow Vortex-Induced Vibrations
03

Offshore Renewable Energy (Wind, Wave)

Analysis of harvesting energy from marine environments. This research investigates the wake evolution and hydrodynamic performance of tidal turbines and wind propulsion systems.

Tidal Turbines Renewable Energy Wake Dynamics
04

Aeroelasticity

Dedicated study of the mutual interaction of aerodynamic, elastic, and inertial forces. Focus on aeroelastic stability of wing systems and hard sails used in wind-assisted ship propulsion.

Stability Analysis SST k-omega Lift/Drag Analysis
